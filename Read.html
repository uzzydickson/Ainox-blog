Civil engineers work on projects ranging from small residential developments to large-scale infrastructure projects, and they are responsible for ensuring that these projects are designed and built in a safe, efficient, and cost-effective manner. Construction management is a field that focuses on the planning, coordination, and control of construction projects. Construction managers, also known as project managers, are responsible for overseeing the construction process from start to finish, including hiring and supervising contractors, coordinating with architects and engineers, and ensuring that projects are completed on time and within budget. Both civil engineering and construction management involve a wide range of tasks and responsibilities, and professionals in these fields often work closely together to complete construction projects. Civil engineers may be involved in the design and planning phase of a project, while construction managers are responsible for implementing the project on site and ensuring that it is completed according to the design plans.
